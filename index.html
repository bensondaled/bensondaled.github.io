Ben Deverett, MD, PhD

Anesthesiologist-scientist innovating at the intersection of brains, machines, and medicine.

Below are project snippets from work at McGill, Carnegie Mellon, Rutgers, Google DeepMind, Princeton, and Stanford.

Email LinkedIn Scholar

Intraoperative AI

Developing and validating machine learning models to unlock the predictive power of intraoperative data and guide clinical decisions (manuscript in preparation, ask me for details).

Transcranial ultrasound neuromodulation

Noninvasively modulating brain circuits with high spatial precision to enhance the way medications influence the brain (study ongoing, ask me for details).

Neuroengineering with anesthetics

Developing software and hardware to perform closed-loop behavioral, pharmacologic, and EEG-based human studies.

Psychoactive medication trials

Running clinical studies to test therapeutic applications of psychedelics and anesthetic medications for new purposes (in peer review and trials ongoing).

Deep RL vs biological intelligence

Characterizing how the cognitive strategies of AI agents compare to those observed in neuroscience (at Google DeepMind).

Neurologic complications in ECMO and cardiac arrest

Identifying risk factors for neurologic injury in patients receiving cardiac mechanical support.

Ketamine neurophysiology

Demonstrating that distinct cortical signatures of ketamine dissociate under wakefulness and anesthesia.

Cerebellar calcium imaging

Studying neuronal encoding rules using cellular-resolution two-photon imaging of the rodent brain.

Optogenetic control of memory

Optogenetically altering neural activity and modeling its effects on memory.

Teaching

Educating Princeton students in programming and math; and Stanford residents on intravenous anesthetics and neuroanatomy.

Global health education

Collaborating with under-resourced medical centers in Guyana, Tanzania, Vietnam, and Rwanda to share clinical and research skills.

Perioperative outcomes

Using multi-institutional operating room data to uncover how anesthesiologist habits influence patient outcomes (in peer review).

Causal inference modeling

Bayesian modeling of human inference strategies for inferring causality between observed events.

PSEyePy

Open-source Python library for high-speed video capture using PlayStation Eye cameras.

Human-computer optimization

Reimagining how we interact with keyboards in iOS to improve efficiency on touchscreens.

Network visualization

Using Amazon Mechanical Turk to study how we can represent measures of centrality in visual graph representations.

EEG musical instrument

Arduino-based MIDI musical instrument based on frontal EEG signals

Music production

Numerous original musical production projects including for an award-winning musical.
